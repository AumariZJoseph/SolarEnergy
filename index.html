Frequently Asked Questions

How long do solar water heaters last?

Solar water heaters typically last 15-20 years with proper maintenance. Our Mega Sun systems come with a 3-year warranty and are designed for durability in Caribbean climates.

Do solar systems work on cloudy days?

Yes! Our evacuated tube technology is designed to capture solar energy even on cloudy days. The vacuum insulation helps maintain heat efficiency in various weather conditions.

How much can I save with solar?

Solar water heaters can reduce your electricity bill by 50-80% for water heating. The exact savings depend on your usage patterns and local energy costs.

How do I choose my water heater?

Choosing the right solar water heater depends on your household size, water usage habits, and whether you use mains or gravity-fed water systems. Selecting the correct size is essential — too large, and the system may overheat or develop leaks; too small, and you may not have enough hot water. A good rule of thumb is to calculate 10 gallons per person in your household. If you frequently host guests, use 12 gallons per person, and if you regularly use hot water for laundry, add about 15 extra gallons. For homes with mains water pressure, a high-pressure solar water heater is recommended. Low-pressure (gravity flow) systems are best suited for specific setups and will not mix properly with mains pressure without additional equipment. If your household will be away for long periods, it’s best to shut off the cold-water supply to the heater and open one or two hot water taps to prevent pressure buildup. Choosing the right system ensures comfort, longevity, and cost efficiency — helping you enjoy reliable hot water year-round.

How often should I service my solar water heater?

All solar water heaters require regular servicing to maintain efficiency and prolong their lifespan. Even if the system seems to be working well, it is recommended to have a general service within the first five years of installation and then every two to three years, depending on your water supply. Systems connected directly to mains water may accumulate sediments and experience corrosion over time, reducing flow and efficiency, while systems using a storage tank with a pressure pump experience less buildup but still require periodic maintenance. Regular servicing helps identify potential issues early, prevents costly repairs, and ensures your solar water heater continues to provide reliable hot water for many years.
